The Order of the Blackened Ash

Region: Southern woodland, transmission disputed
Status: Active
Recognition: Contested by some, respected by others

Founding Claim

The Order maintains that it was formed beneath a lightning-struck tree whose trunk burned without flame for three nights and three days.

They further claim that the practice was brought to the southern woods by a travelling longspooner from the old northern gatherings. Which man this was is disputed.

Character of the Order

  • Difficult, unstable morsels
  • Minimal tolerance for excessive instruction
  • Early failure preferred to prolonged hesitation
  • Quiet before the attempt and sudden noise thereafter

Custom

  • The first morsel is taken in silence
  • No longspooner may speak after shouldering the shaft
  • Shaftsmen advise sparingly and without repetition
  • Laughter, once begun, is not restrained

Known Champions

Edwin Char of the Ash
Remembered for taking a soft fruit cleanly under poor footing while refusing all guidance. Later disgraced for wandering during a lesser bout.

Mara Split-Finger
Noted for an aggressive two-finger Galway, rarely successful but widely witnessed.
