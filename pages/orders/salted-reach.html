The Order of the Salted Reach

Region: Northern coast, said to have stood along the windward reaches
Status: Extinguished, date unknown
Recognition: Exists only in account and contradiction

Account

The Order of the Salted Reach is spoken of in fireside accounts as a northern coastal gathering, said to have stood close to the earliest days following the coming ashore.

No written record of the Order survives. What is known is taken from later retellings, most of which contradict one another in detail while agreeing on tone.

On the Matter of the Open Shore

Some accounts place its gatherings not beneath woodland canopy, but upon the open shore, where wind, salt and shifting ground made the taking of the morsel uncertain even before the attempt began.

Others reject this entirely. They maintain that without canopy, fire and darkness, there can be no true Longspooning.

On Attempts to Restore Shore Play

From time to time, efforts have been made to restore what is claimed to be the practice of the Salted Reach — gatherings held upon open shore, without canopy, and with little or no fire.

Such attempts are rarely well attended. No lasting adoption of shore play has been recorded.

Standing in the Present

The Association records the name. It does not attest to its truth.
