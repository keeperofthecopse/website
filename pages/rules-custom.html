Rules & Custom

The rules of Longspooning are written down because some men require a page before they will accept a judgement.

This does not mean the page is supreme.

The Four Authorities

The Written Rules

The Longspooning Association Rules set out the formal shape of the sport. They exist so that Longspooning may travel without becoming entirely lost. They are useful. They are not enough.

Custom

Custom is what a gathering knows before anyone reads aloud. It varies between Orders. This is tolerated until it becomes dishonest.

The Field

The Field is the gathered body of those who stand and see. It judges the attempt as it happens. There is no appeal beyond the Field.

Eccles Copse

Where the written rule fails, and where the Field is divided, Eccles remembers. The Keepers of the Copse preserve what cannot always be written cleanly.

Disputes

Disputes are settled on the Field. The Association may record the outcome. It does not rehear the matter.

Local Variation

Orders may keep local customs provided the custom does not violate the central principles of the sport. Where local custom attempts such things, it ceases to be custom and becomes excuse.
