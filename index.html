The Public Archive of Longspooning

Shaft. Shoulder. Morsel. Shame.

Longspooning is the anciently disputed and recently documented woodland sport in which a competitor, bearing a longspoon upon the shoulder, must take the morsel by mouth without touching it by hand, biting the spoon, abandoning the shaft, or inviting the disgust of the Field.

The rules are written here for convenience.
They are judged elsewhere.

Notice on the Matter of Recording

For much of its remembered life, Longspooning has resisted all efforts at formal record. After prolonged resistance, and not without objection, the Keepers of the Copse have agreed that a public record may be kept, provided it does not presume to outrank the Field, nor to settle that which is properly disputed in person.

This agreement is not universal. Some Orders have refused outright.

From the Principles

  • No wood, no spoon.
  • The shoulder is sacred.
  • The morsel shall rest, not be secured.
  • The Field judges.
  • Disapproval is enforcement.
  • A bout without witnesses is practice.
